Relay is an **outcome-driven agent orchestrator**. You hand it a single goal —
the _outcome_ — and Relay runs a controlled loop that plans the work, fans it
out across agents, and converges on a critic gate that decides whether the
result is good enough to ship or needs another pass.

Most agent tools ask you to choreograph individual steps. Relay inverts that:
you describe the destination, not the route. The loop figures out the route, and
surfaces the moments where it genuinely needs a human.

## The loop

Every Relay run is the same shape, regardless of the task:

1. An **outcome** enters at the root — the goal you want met.
2. One or more **orchestrators** plan the work and fan it out.
3. **Leaf agents** do the bounded, concrete work in parallel.
4. A **critic gate** judges the converged result: approve, or loop back.

Nothing ships until the critic approves. When it rejects, the signal loops back
to the root and the cycle runs again with what it learned.

## Outcomes, not prompts

An outcome is a durable statement of intent — "the test suite passes and the
changelog is updated" — not a one-shot prompt. Relay keeps working the loop
against that outcome, so a transient failure or a half-finished step doesn't end
the run; it just feeds the next iteration.

<Callout type="note" title="Outcomes are checkable">
  Write outcomes you can verify. "Refactor the auth module" is a vibe; "auth tests pass and no
  public API changed" is an outcome the critic gate can actually rule on.
</Callout>

## The human stays in the loop

Relay never silently resolves a decision that's genuinely yours to make. When a
step needs judgment — approving a deploy, choosing between two valid paths,
rotating a credential — it surfaces that decision and waits. You stay in
control of the calls that matter, without babysitting the ones that don't.

<Callout type="warn" title="Surfaced, never swallowed">
  A correctable mismatch or failure is always raised for you to see. Relay treats "quietly worked
  around it" as a bug, not a feature.
</Callout>
